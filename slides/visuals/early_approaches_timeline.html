Evolution of Deception Theory
1960s
Emotional Leakage
Core Idea: Liars experience guilt/fear → emotions "leak"
Key Finding: Bodily movements leak more than the face
Founders: Ekman & Friesen (1969)
1980s
Cognitive Load
Core Idea: Liars must inhibit truth + invent consistent stories
Key Finding: Deception requires more cognitive resources
Founders: Zuckerman et al. (1981); Hartwig et al. (2014)
Theoretical Shift
From emotional leakage to cognitive load as the primary mechanism of deception detection
↓
Modern Developments
Active elicitation techniques: Strategic questioning, cognitive load, verifiability
Nahari (2019); Vrij & Granhag (2012)
