ADCAT Model: Activation-Decision-Construction-Action Theory
Framework for Understanding Serious Lies
A cognitive framework developed by Walczyk et al. for understanding high-stakes deception through four sequential components
1
ACTIVATION
Truth is automatically activated
Process: Question posed → Truthful response automatically activated in mind
→
2
DECISION
Evaluate: Truth or Deceive?
Factors: Consequences, personal gain, moral implications
→
3
CONSTRUCTION
Formulate plausible falsehood
Goal: Create coherent, believable lie that serves purpose
→
4
ACTION
Deliver while managing cues
Focus: Verbal/nonverbal credibility management
Critical Decision Point: Step 2
CHOOSE TRUTH
Direct response, no additional processing
CHOOSE DECEIT
Proceed to Construction & Action phases
Key Features of ADCAT
• Sequential: Each component builds on the previous
• Cognitive Load: Deception requires additional processing resources
• High Stakes: Designed for serious, consequential lies
• Automatic Activation: Truth activation is involuntary and immediate
Walczyk, J. J., Roper, K. S., Seemann, E., & Humphrey, A. M. (2003). Cognitive mechanisms underlying lying to questions: Response time as a cue to deception. Applied Cognitive Psychology, 17(7), 755-774.
